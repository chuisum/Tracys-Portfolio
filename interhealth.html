Project: InterHealth (Mobile)

Purpose-It helps eliminate waiting time on the phone, and avoid unnecessary in-person visits. It doesn’t only benefits people who are with busy working schedules also people with disabilities. It is like an all-in-one.

My Process

Research Methods

User interviews & User surveys' insights

  • Time is valuable to all users, they want to have one app to skip all the waiting, including waiting on doctors replies, waiting on the phone just to book an appointment, waiting for available appointments, etc.
  • Have an app that allows users to just see which clinic/hospital is covered by their policy/insurance. That will skip the research time of the user.

User interviews & User surveys' goal

  • Identify what features are useful and why.
  • Identify the obstacles user faces and what makes them do not want to use an app.

Mental Models

These two groups are representing a younger generation who are occupied with work-the workaholics, and the retired generation who doesn’t know too much about tech. Mental models capture one persona at a time to determine what types of motivations might be driving their behaviors. These two diagrams pointed out their pain points and identified their why's.

User flows

These user flows were conducted after I created the site map following user’s liking. There are three main features which are shown below.

1- Under My Health, there are three different tabs, and one of them is the record. Choose a record to share.

2- To make an appointment can either hit the shortcut icon from the home page or under the My Health, appointment tab.

3- To read a message click on the message. Choose any message to read.

Takeaways

One of the many challenges I faced was not being able to withdraw myself from putting in too many details on a low-fidelity wireframe and wasted a lot of time trying to be perfect without realizing errors were all I have drawn at that point. During the design process, after each time of conducting surveys and interviews, I always find myself changing the app features a little bit, for example, it went from five tabs on the navigation bar to four, because most participants voted some tabs were useless and duplicated. This is why conducting research is important because designers seem to get shifted away from their original design purpose when they are designing.
